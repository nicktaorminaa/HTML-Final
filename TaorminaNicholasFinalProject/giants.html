History

The New York Giants are a professional American football team based in the New York metropolitan area. The Giants compete in the National Football League (NFL) as a member club of the league's National Football Conference (NFC) East division. The team plays its home games at MetLife Stadium in East Rutherford, New Jersey, which it shares with the New York Jets in a unique arrangement. The Giants hold their summer training camp at the Quest Diagnostics Training Center at the Meadowlands Sports Complex.

The Giants were one of five teams that joined the NFL in 1925, and is the only one of that group still existing, as well as the league's longest-established team in the Northeastern United States. Stadium

The Giants play their home games at MetLife Stadium, located in East Rutherford, New Jersey. The stadium, which opened in 2010, has a seating capacity of 82,500 and is known for its state-of-the-art amenities and comfortable seating. It is also home to the New York Jets and has hosted numerous major events, including Super Bowls XLVIII and XLIX.

Fans

Giants fans are known for their passionate support of the team. From tailgating before the game to cheering on the Giants from home, fans play a crucial role in the team's success. The Giants have a strong fan base not only in the New York metropolitan area, but across the country and around the world.
